FRACTALS

A fractal is a structure composed of parts that are in some sense similar to the whole. Have you ever seen a fern? This is a kind of natural fractal.

If you are curious, there are different types of fractals.
1. Geometric fractals.
2. Algebraic fractals (some of which you can try in this program)
3. Scholastic fractals.

You can read more about all kinds of fractals here: https://bitly.su/hSmueGD

Now back to the fractals in Leben:
1. To start with click on the “Fractals” button in the main menu.

2. Choose between Mandelbrot or Julia, enter parameters!
Zoom – it`s distance to fractal. Higher zoom makes scope wider.
С – complex constant value, with respect to which we consider these fractals.
Mandelbrot set – z(n+1) = z2 + c
Julia set z=zi +c

3. Pick color scheme!

4. Click "Generate" to create fractal.

COLOR SCHEMES

A color model is an abstract model for describing color representation in the form of tuples (sets) of numbers, usually of three or four values, called color components or color coordinates.

The most popular schemes are:
- RGB
- CMYK
- HSV
- XYZ
- HSL

Leben gives you opportunity to work with RGB, CMYK and HSV color scheme.

1. click on open file button and open your image file.
Now you have 2 pictures.Why 2? Because you are working with 2 different color schemes : HSV and CMYK.

2. Pick some C - value, click on Convert and look at the second picture.

What`s happened with it? You manually chose amount of blue color on the picture.
C - value - amount of Cyan color (CMYK is Cyan, Magenta, Yellow and BlacK).

AFFINE TRANSFORMATION

Affine transformation is a linear mapping method that preserves points, straight lines, and planes. Sets of parallel lines remain parallel after an affine transformation.

There are 3 main types of affine transformations for movies:
1. Offset
2. Zooming
3. Rotate

Our program shows affine transformations in the example of a square.
Firstly, let’s enter the coordinates of the diagonal to draw a square.
Then you can pick type of affine transformation, or 2, or all of them, it`s not a problem. (don`t forget to click on checkboxes)
But it can be not such easy because you need also enter some parameters for each option, and I`ll try to briefly tell you about all of them.

1. Offset - determines how much you move the square relative to the [0;0] point.
x - offset related to x-axis
y - offset related to y-axis.

2. Scale - determines the scaling of the square.
a - coefficient for x
d - coefficient for y
x`= x*a;
y`=y*d;
x`, y` - new coordinates after scaling.

3. Rotate - determines angle of square rotating in degrees.

Looking simple, isn`t it?
